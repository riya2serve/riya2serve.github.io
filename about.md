Welcome, I am excited to meet you here!

I was raised in Sacramento, California, where early exposure to local ecosystems and environmental issues sparked my interest in biology. This curiosity evolved into a passion for ecological and evolutionary research, which I pursued as an undergraduate at Mills College. At Mills I investigated predator-prey dynamics, landscape influences on benthic invertebrate settlement, and marine fouling communities. I further expanded my interests through an NSF REU at the Smithsonian Environmental Research Center (SERC), where I studied latitudinal gradients in community composition and predation pressure.

Following graduation, I entered a post-baccalaureate research program based at Columbia University, during which I joined The Bendesky Lab at the Zuckerman Mind Brain Behavior Institute. There, I investigated the genetic and molecular basis of hybrid incompatibilities in *Peromyscus* mice, contributing to ongoing studies on the large X effect, imprinting disruption, and hybrid overgrowth. This work integrated embryo collection, next-generation sequencing, and genome-wide expression analysis, solidifying my interests in genomics and developmental biology.

I am currently a Master’s student in the Department of Ecology, Evolution, and Environmental Biology (E3B) at Columbia University, where I study the evolutionary and genetic mechanisms underlying hybridization in dioecious Amaranthus species. My research in the Eaton Lab focuses on segregation distortion in amaranth hybrids—a promising system given Amaranthus’s complex reproductive strategies and phylogenetic history.

Alongside my research, I remain committed to equity in STEM and community engagement. As a post-baccalaureate researcher, I served as a mentor community leader for the New York Academy of Sciences, supporting teams of scientists and educators working with under-resourced schools across New York City. Now as a graduate student, I actively contribute to the E3B Outreach Committee and the E3Buddies mentorship program, helping organize departmental events such as NSF-GRFP workshops and career panels.

Beyond the department, I serve on the board of Women in Science at Columbia (WISC), where I help facilitate mentorship and support for undergraduate and graduate students across the Morningside campus. I also bring experience in science policy and environmental advocacy, having contributed to legislative efforts introduced by the California Ocean Protection Council and non-profit, NextGen Policy.

Outside of academia, I enjoy distance running, swimming, cooking, and reading.
